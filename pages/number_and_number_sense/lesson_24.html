Lesson 24: Performing Series of Operations (MDAS) Explore and Discover!

Look at the number sentence below.

2 x 3 ÷ 4 = N

35 - 6 x 3 = N

7 x 9 ÷ 8 = N

18 - 12 ÷ 6 + 7 = N

12 - 3 ÷ 6 x 7 = N

Have you encountered problems like these?

How did you get the answer?

How many operations are there in each problem?

Study the following:

To solve these problems, you must use a rule to arrive at the correct answer.

Here, the MDAS rule must be followed strictly to get the correct answer.

The MDAS stands for the 4 basic operations --- Multiplication, Division, Addition, Subtraction.

The rules say that: Multiply or divide first in the order as they come from left to right then, add or subtract in order as they come from left to right.

Remember: Do Multiplication or Division first before you do Addition or Subtraction
